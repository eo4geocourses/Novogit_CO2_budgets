What is a CO2 budget?

  • A CO2 budget (carbon budget) is the upper limit of CO2 emissions in order to remain below a specific average global temperature
  • Please note that CO2 emissions are accumulated in the atmosphere. This means that overspending one year has to be compensated the following years
  • There is almost a linear relationship between global temperature rise and cumulative CO2 emissions
  • The global CO2 budget can be split in space and time, for instance as a national CO2 budget for the period 2020 – 2050
  • The budget usually also include a plan on how to reach the goals
  • The paris agreement has a two-headed temperature goal. It pledge to keep the average global surface temperature “well below 2 °C” and “pursue efforts” to limit the increase since preindustrial times to 1.5 °c

Basic conversion rules



  • CO2 emission data is usually shown in billion tonnes CO2 (GtCO2)
  • 1 Gigatonne (Gt) = 1 billion tonnes = 1 Petagram (Pg)
  • 1 kg carbon (C) = 3.664 kg carbon dioxide (CO2)
  • 1 GtC = 3.664 billion tonnes CO2 = 3.664 GtCO2

  • The united nations entity supporting the global response to climate change
  • Has nearly universal membership (197 parties)
  • Is the parent treaty of the 2015 Paris agreement, and the parent treaty of the 1997 Kyoto protocol and its Doha amendment, adopted in 2012.
  • EU member states reports yearly emissions to UNFCCC. Sectoral data according to the IPCC classification are also delivered

CO2 budgets - Summary



  • A CO2 budget (carbon budget) is the upper limit of CO2 emissions in order to remain below a specific average global temperature
  • The global CO2 budget can be split in space and time, for instance as a national CO2 budget for the period 2020 – 2050
  • A CO2 budget may include the current status, targets and goals, planned actions and their impacts and monitoring and indicators
  • According to the ranking of Swedish municipalities link, 31 (13 %) Swedish municipalities have established CO2 budgets, 61 (25 %) are working on it and 149 (62 %) have not yet started. No reply excluded from the statistics.
  • CO2 budgets are considered to be the major scientific tool to estimate the amount of reductions in CO2 emission required in order to reach the goals of the Paris agreement
  • By using CO2 budgets, the municipality administration can identify the most efficient actions

Satellite navigation (SatNav)

The European SatNav programme is called Galileo. With EGNOS, the European differential global positioning system, it belongs to the world spanning global navigation satellite systems (GNSS).


Galileo-enabled smartphones can be used in many different applications.

Satellite communication (SatCom)

SatCom allows telecommunication all around the globe. Communication satellites receive signals from Earth and retransmit them by a transponder.


The satellite as a self-contained communications system, the example of IntelSat.
